01 — About

A little
about me.

Software engineer with a strong open-source background. Currently mentoring at the Wikimedia Foundation and contributing to tools used by millions of people worldwide.

I'm Ademola Omosanya, a software engineer based in Lagos, Nigeria. My journey into engineering started with a curiosity about how things on the internet are built — and it hasn't stopped since.

My open-source story began with OCaml and grew into a meaningful internship at the Wikimedia Foundation through Outreachy, where I contributed to the MediaWiki VisualData extension. I stayed on as a volunteer, then returned as a mentor for the next generation of contributors.

I care about writing clean, maintainable code and collaborating in communities that make technology more accessible. I enjoy reviewing PRs, participating in technical discussions, and helping developers find their footing in large open-source projects.

When I'm not building things, I'm learning — whether that's a new framework, a design pattern, or how other engineers approach hard problems.
